# The Impact of the CHIPS Act on Domestic Supply Chains

The recent legislation aimed at bolstering domestic semiconductor manufacturing provides an estimated $52 billion in subsidies to leading silicon fabricators. 

## Supply Chain Resilience
By migrating leading-edge node production (e.g., 3nm and below) to domestic foundries, the dependency on global geopolitical chokepoints is drastically reduced. This aligns with broader structural policy shifts designed to secure critical technological infrastructure over the next decade.

## AI Infrastructure Deployment
This macroeconomic strategy directly impacts AI deployment capabilities. As high-end GPUs become computationally denser, the specialized silicon substrates required rely heavily on robust, unhindered supply chains. Localized manufacturing ensures that next-generation embodied AI models and large language models maintain steady hardware integration cycles.
